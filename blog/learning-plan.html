Learning plan

My long term plan

I've been wanting to be able to develop my own products that will find enough of a market that I could live off them, and hopefully spend little enough time maintaining and marketing them that I can have more time for other, non-money-making pursuits - like making art, reading books, going on long walks and learning new things.

If I could find some happy intersection of economic measurement (i.e. related to my current job in inflation measurement) and art (i.e. related to what I like to do in my spare time) then that would be the dream! Perhaps an art-investment tool that applies price index methods to resale of investment art. Perhaps a social networking tool that enables emerging artists to buy each other’s work. Perhaps a tool that applies AI to macro-economic data to produce artworks tracking the economy.

Failing that, I have some ideas that sit firmly on one side or the other like: a data privacy tool that produces synthetic data on the fly for analysts who want to access confidential data, or a remote art-making site where users can provide specs to artists who make work in response to those.

My learning strengths and limitations

One strength I have is that I’m good at seeing connections across different subjects, which means I can often apply simple ideas in new ways. On the flipside, I’m limited by being interested in lots of things at once, which can make it hard to devote enough time to any one of them! It can be hard to focus for long enough and hard enough to make as much progress as I’d like.

Developing core skills

Getting better at managing my time. Learning how to balance work and play so that I don’t burn myself out. Communicating well – supportively and constructively – with my fellow students. Keeping on track with following the curriculum while still allowing time and space for following up things I’m interested in (allowing myself to fall down a few interesting rabbit holes, but ... not too far or for too long)

Managing my workload

I commit to spending regular blocks of time on the work in the programme, planning at the start of the week how much of the content I need to have covered by the end of each day. I aim to come onsite to Dev Academy at least twice a week to work undistracted by home-stuff and to meet some of the other students. When and if things start building up and getting to me I’ll take a break for a few hours and go for a long walk, and if I’m still struggling I’ll get in touch with a facilitator to talk it through.

Seeking help

I will let others know if I need help, after first searching Discord to see if similar issues have already been discussed.

The facilitation team

I'd like the facilitators to be supportive, available (within reason) and enthusiastic.

Other commitments

During foundations I will generally busy in the morning (working reduced hours to wrap up loose ends at my job, as enrolling at Dev Academy was a spur-of-the-moment decision) so afternoons, evenings and weekends will be the times I devote to the course. I’ll be checking Discord regularly during the morning in case anything urgent pops up. My hours focusing on foundations, then, will be 1pm – 6pm consistently through the week, and then more flexibly during the evenings (1-2 hours) and weekends (3-4 hours on each of Saturday and Sunday).

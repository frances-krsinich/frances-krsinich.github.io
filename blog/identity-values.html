Identity, values and strengths

A past ethical decision

I left a higher-paying job at a policy agency a few years ago to return to Stats NZ, because I realised that I wanted to be working on things with wider application and greater positive impact for society.

The influence of my culture and the people around me

I left school after sixth form in the 80's with the explicit goal of going on the dole and being an artist and musician like my (older) friends. After a few years of playing music and having no money in Christchurch I found myself slowly spiralling into a poverty trap, which motivated me to study. After a year of mechanical engineering at Polytech I returned to my roots (my grandfather and favorite, most influential, relative was a statistician) and switched to a maths degree.

Coming out as queer in the 80's meant being part of a small, largely invisible, subculture which was both exciting, strange and ultimately freeing. My children now live in a totally different world which is awesome to see.

A big influence over the last few years has been a computer scientist called Cal Newport, who has written great books on topics like 'Digital Minimalism' (basically, get off social media) and 'Deep Work' (essentially, how to focus on things and do stuff like timeboxing)

My strengths

My strengths are enthusiasm, communication and making connections across different subjects and methods. I'm good at identifying and drawing out other people's interests and skills, and being encouraging.

My limitations

My limitations are occasional impatience, being interested in too many things at once, and having to earn enough money to live on (which means I have less time for stuff other than paid-working)

A past experience of resistance or tension

Over the last year or so there has been tension at work between another team and ours, on a project to replace some old (very old! 25 years) technology. I ended up holding the short straw as the middle-man between the teams (aka the acting product owner) and so this has required lots of diplomacy, communication and humour. I spend quite a bit of time and effort translating each side to the other and providing reassurance to those in my team that are worried they'll loose the functionality they need. It's important to keep everyone as engaged as possible as it will be a multi-year project that requires ongoing effort from us to make sure the redevelopment team understands what we need.

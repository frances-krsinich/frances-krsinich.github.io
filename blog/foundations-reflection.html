Reflections on foundations

Three new things

Three new things I've learnt about myself and my ego via the core learnings in foundations:

1. I tend to jump ahead and dive into things rather than taking the time to read all instructions carefully and the return to reading them - this is likely due to a form of arrogance about thinking I already know what to do, so more humility is in order!

2. I need to realise that my brain can still rewire and learn new things. That the difficulty and frustration that I experience when learning new things is a sign that the brain-muscles are building and it is something to be embraced rather than avoided.

3. I can be a better listener to others by sitting back and just... listening. Rather than focussing on what and how I can contribute to what they're saying. This is a much better, more compassionate and ultimately more useful way of listening.

The role of values, empathy and self-awareness

Self awareness means that I can be more conscious of what's going on in my mind and body when I'm learning, and more consciously notice when I might be avoiding certain topics due to difficulty or frustration. Then I can be more deliberate about choosing to stay with the task, while taking regular breaks, to learn better.

Empathy is important for understanding that we're all at different points on our learning journey, and with different backgrounds and experiences that we're bringing to the table. Everyone has their own set of challenges and skills and being aware of this helps us to be more responsive and sensitive when communicating and being with each other.

Values are what motivate me to put the time and effort into learning and developing during this course. I strongly value my ability to create new and useful things, and keeping that front of mind helps to provide the context for why I'm here and why I want to be giving it full attention.

What's surprised me?

The importance of these core skills to being a good programmer has surprised me but it makes a lot of sense. In my current job at Stats NZ (and previously at other organisations) it's really clear that the people with good communication skills, self awareness and a clear set of values underpinning why they're in the job, is much more important than what technical skills they happen to possess.

The most challenging aspects of core so far

The most challenging aspects of core have been prioritising it alongside the tech material. While I know it's important, it tends to get shuffled down the list of what I need to do, in comparison with the more tangible material of the tech part of the course.

It's also been challenging to realise that I may not be as good a communicator as I had assumed, and also that I have a lot to learn and need to deal usefully with the frustrations of not 'getting' things straight away. Being patient with myself is not something that comes naturally to me!

Why so much focus on core skills?

It makes complete sense to me that Dev Academy focusses on core skills so much. This is probably the point of difference between the course and other web development bootcamps. I can see that it is a very useful skill to be nurturing in students in response to what industry wants to see in the new recruits they're looking for.

Was it a waste of time?

Not at all! I think the core material has been a really crucial part of the course so far and I look forward to learning more during the bootcamp in this area. In particular I appreciate the extra resources that we've been pointed to along the way in the core curriculum.

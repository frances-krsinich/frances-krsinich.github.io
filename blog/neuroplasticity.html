Neuroplasticity and the growth mindset

Neuroplasticity

Understanding neuropasticity benefits people by enabling them to reinterpret effort and hard work. Rather than seeing these as an indication that 'they're not intelligent enough' to understand or achieve, they can reframe things to see effort and hard work as an indication that their brains are undergoing change into being more capable of doing the task or understanding the information.

This is analogous to building muscle at the gym. The strain and work required to lift weights leads to the building of muscle.

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset, as opposed to a fixed mindset, is one which recognises that the brain is malleable, and can change and develop in response to mental effort. That is, it understandss neuropasticity, and utilises this understanding by seeing difficulty as a way to develop new skills.

This is highly relevant to us at Dev Academy because most of us are engaged in learning totally new concepts, and this learning is difficult, challenging and at times frustrating. By understanding that this difficulty is leading to the development of new pathways in our brains, which will make those skills easier over time, we are motivated to persevere.

I was surprised that physical activities like playing piano or even building muscle could be enhanced by mental practice. This opens up the possibility for me that I might not bother joining the gym after all, but instead lie on the couch and imagine lifting weights. Win-win!

I checked this out further, and found this good article which refers to an impressive study (I saw the same study referred to in quite a few different articles) that tested a control group against a group to visualise flexing their muscles - the test group increased their strength by 35% and also strengthened the corresponding neural pathways in their brains.

The relevance for me of all this is that I will view the difficulties and occasional frustrations of the course as growth opportunities, rather than as indications that I'm not up to the job!

Impact on my learning plan and strategies

The takeaway from all this, for me, is that I will consciously try to challenge myself more. Research and read material that is at the edge of (or even beyond) what I feel capable of understanding. And attempt the stretch excercises as a matter of course.

Also, I wasn't joking, I really am going to try and build muscle by doing the imagining thing - this really appeals!
